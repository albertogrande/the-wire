# Reader Taste

Agent-maintained. Durable preferences only — update when the reader gives
feedback (in session or via issue comments); prune one-off reactions.

## Voice

- Lee Robinson's clarity × The Pragmatic Engineer's depth. Short sentences,
  simple words, short paragraphs. Depth from numbers and primary sources,
  never from rhetorical flourish.
- Rejected: the heavier Stratechery-style register (long thesis sentences,
  dramatic constructions like "Sit with the symmetry"). The reader "didn't
  fully connect" with it.
- Approved sample of the target voice: the 2026-06-12 trust-stack dive.

## Format

- Weekly = ONE essay deciding what mattered; beats are research lenses,
  never output sections. The reader explicitly does not want per-topic news
  lists.
- Short "Also this week" footer (4–8 one-liners) is wanted.
- A deep dive every week alongside the essay; reader may also request
  topic-specific dives on demand.
- A shorter daily dive (Tue–Sun) under a rotating columnist byline
  (`AUTHORS.md`) — evergreen "how it works" by default, the day's news only
  when it earns a durable explainer. Sundays run a three-columnist bakeoff;
  record which voice the reader prefers here as the experiment settles.
- Cross-domain connection (e.g. AI × energy × economy × politics) is the
  core value: "figure out which points of the week relate to another topic
  or broader trends — here is where you become opinionated."

## Coverage

- Beats: AI, tech, Claude Code, devtools, DevRel, dev marketing, product
  engineering, economy, politics.
- Claude Code is a daily tool for the reader — small news there can
  outrank big news elsewhere.
- Discussions (HN/Reddit/X) matter to the reader as much as news.
